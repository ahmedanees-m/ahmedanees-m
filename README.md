## About Me

I'm a **PhD candidate at VIT Vellore** working at the intersection of **synthetic biology**, **machine learning**, and **quantum computing**. My research focuses on building computational tools that make genome engineering programmable, predictable, and scalable.

I design and engineer genome-writing enzymes (CRISPR/Cas, base editors, recombinases), build ML models to predict and optimize their behavior, and develop quantum-classical hybrid algorithms to explore sequence design problems that classical compute struggles with.

> *Trained in the wet lab. Now engineering the code that engineers genomes.*

---

## Research Focus

| Domain | What I Do |
|---|---|
| **CRISPR & Genome Writing** | Engineering TnsC (ShCAST), base editors (ABEs), and recombinases for programmable DNA insertion |
| **Machine Learning for Biology** | XGBoost/CNN models for base editor efficiency prediction; GNNs for enzyme discovery with ESM-2 embeddings |
| **Quantum-Classical Hybrid** | Quantum circuits for DNA thermodynamics encoding and regulatory sequence design (Qiskit, PennyLane) |
| **Synthetic Biology Pipelines** | Automated library design, scoring, and benchmarking for recombinase/editor engineering |
| **Chromatin & Hi-C Analysis** | Chromatin contact prediction and CRISPR off-target mapping using ML |
